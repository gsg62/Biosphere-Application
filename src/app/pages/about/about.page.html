Glossary:

  • Biomass density: Density of organisms in a particular area
  • Autotroph biomass density: Density of organisms who use chemical processes to obtain energy and nutrients. Examples: algae and grass
  • Carnivore biomass density: Density of organisms whose diet consists mainly of animals. Examples: bears and tigers
  • Deciduous biomass density: Density of plants that shed their leaves on a yearly basis. Examples: oak and maple trees
  • Ectotherm biomass density: Density of organism who reply on their environment to regulate body temperature. Examples: fishes and reptiles
  • Endotherm biomass density: Density of organisms who can regulate their own body temperature. Examples: humans and birds
  • Evergreen biomass density: Density of plants that never lose all their leaves at one time. Examples: pine and spruce trees
  • Herbivore biomass density: Density of organisms whose diet consists primarily of plants. Examples: elephants and cows
  • Iteroparity biomass density: Density of animals or plants that reproduce multiple times. Examples: humans and birds
  • Omnivore biomass density: Density of organisms whose diet consists of animals and plants. Examples: most turtles and chimpanzees
  • Semelparity biomass density: Density of animals and plants who reproduce only once in their life cycle. Examples: annual plants and most spiders

  • Abundance density: Frequency of organisms in a specified region
  • Carnivore abundance density: Frequency of organisms who mainly eat animals. Examples: bears and tigers
  • Ectotherm abundance density: Frequency of organisms who can't regulate their body temperature on their own. Examples: fishes and reptiles
  • Endotherm abundance density: Frequency of organisms who regulate their own body temperature. Examples: humans and birds
  • Herbivore abundance density: Frequency of organisms who eat mainly plants. Examples: elephants and cows
  • Iteroparity abundance density: Frequency of organisms that reproduce multiple times. Examples: humans and birds
  • Omnivore abundance density: Frequency of organisms who eat both plants and animals. Examples: most turtles and chimpanzees
  • Semelparity abundance density: Frequency of organisms who reproduce on only once before they die. Examples: annual plants and most spiders
