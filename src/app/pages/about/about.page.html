Glossary:

  • Biomass density: Biomass of organisms in a particular area
  • Autotroph biomass density: Biomass of organisms which typically rely on photosynthesis (sometimes on chemosynthesis) and thus do not rely on consuming other organisms. Examples: algae and grass
  • Carnivore biomass density: Biomass of organisms whose diet consists mainly of animals. Examples: bears and tigers
  • Deciduous biomass density: Biomass of plants that shed their leaves on a yearly basis. Examples: oak and maple trees
  • Ectotherm biomass density: Biomass of organism who rely on their environment to regulate body temperature. Examples: fishes and reptiles
  • Endotherm biomass density: Biomass of organisms who regulate their own body temperature. Examples: humans and birds
  • Evergreen biomass density: Biomass of plants that never lose all their leaves at one time. Examples: pine and spruce trees
  • Herbivore biomass density: Biomass of organisms whose diet consists primarily of plants. Examples: elephants and cows
  • Iteroparity biomass density: Biomass of organisms that reproduce multiple times through their lifetime. Examples: humans and birds
  • Omnivore biomass density: Biomass of organisms whose diet consists of animals and plants. Examples: most turtles and chimpanzees
  • Semelparity biomass density: Biomass of organisms who reproduce only once through their lifetime. Examples: annual plants and most spiders

  • Abundance density: Frequency of organisms in a specified region
  • Carnivore abundance density: Frequency of organisms whose diet consists mainly of animals. Examples: bears and tigers
  • Ectotherm abundance density: Frequency of organisms who rely on their environment to regulate body temperature. Examples: fishes and reptiles
  • Endotherm abundance density: Frequency of organisms who regulate their own body temperature. Examples: humans and birds
  • Herbivore abundance density: Frequency of organisms whose diet consists primarily of plants. Examples: elephants and cows
  • Iteroparity abundance density: Frequency of organisms that reproduce multiple times through their lifetime. Examples: humans and birds
  • Omnivore abundance density: Frequency of organisms whose diet consists of animals and plants. Examples: most turtles and chimpanzees
  • Semelparity abundance density: Frequency of organisms who reproduce only once through their lifetime. Examples: annual plants and most spiders
